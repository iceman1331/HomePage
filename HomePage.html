Diabetes: A chronic disease has impacted over 463 million ppl worldwide.

The BIG PIE

Since the 1920s, diabetes treatments have depended on expensive and invasive insulin injections.

We propose The BIG PIE which intends to tackle diabetes without insulin injections. A cheap, non-invasive alternative to injections. Our project is based on the principle of “transdifferentiation” where we transform one somatic cell type into another type. We intend to reprogram a subset of gut cells into b-islet-like cells, capable of glucose-responsive insulin secretion. This has previously done with viruses. But due to safety and health concerns, we have replaced viral vectors with E.coli Nissle a probiotic bacteria that will serve as our delivery vector.
